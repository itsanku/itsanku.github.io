West Bengal Teacher Recruitment Scam & SSC Protest (2025)

Comprehensive Overview, Timeline, and Referenced Facts

Background: The Recruitment Scam

In 2016, the West Bengal School Service Commission (WBSSC) conducted recruitment for 24,640 sanctioned teaching and non-teaching posts. However, 25,753 appointment letters were issued, raising suspicions of large-scale irregularities. Investigations revealed manipulation of OMR answer sheets, fabricated merit lists, and appointments allegedly secured through bribes, bypassing merit entirely.[6]

2016: WBSSC conducts recruitment for 24,640 posts; 23 lakh candidates apply; 25,753 appointments made.[6]
2022: High-profile arrests and CBI/ED probe begin.
April 3, 2025: Supreme Court upholds Calcutta High Court's annulment of all 25,753 appointments, citing "systemic failure" and fraud.[6][4][5]
April 17, 2025: Supreme Court allows over 13,000 'untainted' assistant teachers to continue until December 31, 2025, but orders fresh recruitment.[2][4][5][6]
April 21-22, 2025: Over 2,000 teachers protest outside WBSSC office, demanding publication of 'tainted' and 'untainted' lists.[1][3]

Supreme Court Orders & Current Status

  • All 25,753 appointments from 2016 recruitment cancelled due to "widespread irregularities" and "fraudulent manipulation" in the process.[4][5][6]
  • Over 13,000 'untainted' assistant teachers allowed to remain in service until December 31, 2025, with no special rights in new recruitment.[2][4][5][6]
  • Fresh recruitment process must be advertised by May 31, 2025, and completed by December 31, 2025.[2][4][5][6]
  • Group C and D staff (where about 50% were found tainted) are excluded from this relief.[2][5][6]
  • Tainted candidates must refund salaries received as their appointments were "the result of fraud".[6]

Teacher Protests at WBSSC Office

Over 2,000 teachers who lost their jobs due to the Supreme Court order have been protesting outside the WBSSC office in Salt Lake, Kolkata, demanding the immediate release of lists distinguishing 'untainted' (merit-based) and 'tainted' (bribe-based) appointees.[3]

"We have stayed here under the open sky since last evening and will continue to be here until our demands are met."
— Protester, Business Standard[3]

Protesters have braved harsh conditions, with many expressing frustration at the lack of state support and basic facilities. Some residents have opened their homes for restroom use and brought food and water, showing community solidarity.[1]

"The state has not cooperated with us in this heat. We are sitting here, sleeping here, leaving our families behind. We are not drinking water because there are no bio-toilets. We can be dehydrated and fall ill but still, the government is not concerned. But we are grateful that some of the homes here have allowed us to use their toilets."
— Woman protestor, Indian Express[1]
"They are the spine of society; they prepare the future generations. Now they are out fighting for their rights, so we all should stand by them."
— Avinash Mondol, local supporter[1]
  • Immediate release of 'untainted' and 'tainted' candidate lists
  • Transparency in the OMR sheet evaluation process
  • Resignation of Education Minister and SSC Chairman
  • Compensation for lost jobs (demanded by some protesters)

Government and WBSSC Response

  • WBSSC Chairman Siddhartha Majumdar stated, "SSC will abide by the orders of the Hon'ble Supreme Court."[3]
  • State Education Minister Bratya Basu urged teachers to wait for a review petition, cautioning that publishing lists may not withstand legal scrutiny.[3]
  • Chief Minister Mamata Banerjee assured teachers, "We are happy with the Supreme Court. There is a sense of relief. I would request teachers not worry. We will resolve the issues."[4]
